Research topics

I am a PhD candidate in weather based forecasting and decision making for microgrids at the University of Liège (Belgium). Previously, I held positions in several compagnies of the energy field (nuclear power plant, energy manager of an energy supplier, etc), see more at Linkedin. I am deeply interested in making research collaborations. See Google-scholar for the publications.

My reasearch is at the intersection of machine learning and power systems and includes:

  • probabilistic forecasting applied to power systems: PV, wind power, consumption, electricity prices ...
  • stochastic and robust optimization
  • deep generative models applied to power systems: VAE, GAN, NF ...
  • local energy communities
  • energy management systems
  • modelling future energy systems including EROI, carbon footprint constraints

Volunteering activities

I am an active member of the Shifters Belgium and of the working group on sustainability of the Phd candidates of Liège university ReD. The Shifters Belgium is an ngo that helps to make the energy transition towards a carbon-free economy. In particular it aims to provide punctual support to The Shift Project ngo, to inform, to discuss, and to share the key ways of achieving a carbon-free transition. I also strongly support ClimActes that is a summer school on climate change to build projects on mobility, education, energy, etc to help to achieve IPCC targets.

My volunteering activities include:

  • speaker for the conference teach the Shift Belgium
  • lobbying to public institutions on the climate change and how to make the energy transition towards a carbon-free economy
  • facilitator for the climate collage and digital collage
